Methodology: PSD2 / UK SCA context: Under PSD2 in the EU and the FCA's SCA requirements in the UK, Strong Customer Authentication is mandatory for all in-scope card-not-present transactions. There is no legitimate non-authenticated baseline for EU/UK issuers — every in-scope transaction must be authenticated (ECI 05/02) or covered by a valid exemption (TRA, low-value under €30/£30, trusted beneficiary, recurring, MIT). This model therefore frames the comparison as: step-up challenge baseline (current state, before Outseer) vs Outseer frictionless TRA exemption (target state). Both paths result in ECI 05/02 — the issuer carries liability in both cases. The saving is driven by operational efficiency (fewer challenges, lower fraud rate on scored frictionless volume), not by authentication vs non-authentication.

Inbound fraud claim processing: Both step-up challenged and frictionless transactions are ECI 05/02 — the issuer has no dispute rights against the merchant on either. The saving here reflects that Outseer's risk engine concentrates fraud attempts into the step-up pool, so frictionless transactions generate fewer inbound cardholder fraud claims per transaction. Non-fraud disputes (goods not received, not as described) are unaffected by 3DS and excluded throughout. ECI 06/01 (attempted auth) claim processing is conservatively excluded.

OTP & OOB challenge channels: OTP (SMS one-time passcode) and OOB (Out-of-Band push notification via mobile banking app) are modelled separately. Only the OTP portion of challenged transactions incurs per-message SMS gateway fees — OOB push delivery via APNS/FCM carries near-zero marginal cost. Cart abandonment is weighted across both channels: OOB push typically has lower abandonment than OTP because the cardholder approves within their banking app without needing to switch apps or wait for an SMS. The blended abandonment rate is: (OTP split × OTP abandonment rate) + (OOB split × OOB abandonment rate).

Interchange: Under PSD2, interchange is capped at 0.20% (consumer debit) and 0.30% (consumer credit) regardless of authentication method — there is no differential between frictionless and challenged transactions. The interchange rate in this model is used only to calculate income on transactions recovered from challenge screen abandonment (Line 4). Select your portfolio card type to apply the correct PSD2 cap. All figures are illustrative — calibrate to your portfolio actuals.

Fraud detection rate & loss saving: The fraud saving is calculated from the improvement in fraud detection rate, defined as: Detection Rate = Confirmed Fraud (case-marked) / (Confirmed Fraud + Missed Fraud). Confirmed fraud = cases created and marked as fraud in the fraud case management system. Missed fraud = fraud discovered retrospectively via cardholder repudiation or the chargeback file — false negatives the system did not catch. The saving = (missed fraud before − missed fraud after) × net fraud loss per missed transaction. Volumes are entered for the chosen data period and annualised automatically. Both confirmed and missed fraud volumes result in ECI 05/02 transactions under PSD2/SCA mandatory authentication — the issuer bears the full fraud loss on all missed cases with no recovery route from the merchant. The "remaining missed fraud loss" shows the residual annual loss on cases Outseer still does not detect, keeping the model honest about what is not yet caught.
